Copernicus HR-VPP Vegetation Phenology

Executive Summary

High Resolution Vegetation Phenology and Productivity (HR-VPP) from the Copernicus Land Monitoring Service. Provides 13 phenological metrics per pixel at 10m resolution across Europe (EEA-39), derived from Sentinel-2 imagery. Covers two growing seasons per year from 2017 to 2024. Key metrics include start/end of season dates, peak greenness, season length, and seasonal productivity.

CoverageEurope (EEA-39)
Resolution10m (Sentinel-2)
Temporal2017-2024 (annual)
LicenseCopernicus Data Policy (free, open)

Description

HR-VPP derives vegetation phenology parameters from Sentinel-2 Plant Phenology Index (PPI) time series at 10m resolution. PPI is computed from Sentinel-2 L2A surface reflectance using the relationship between near-infrared and red bands, corrected for soil and atmospheric effects. A smoothed seasonal trajectory is fitted to the dekadal (10-day) PPI composites, from which phenological metrics are extracted for up to two growing seasons per year. Available metrics per season: Start of Season Date (SOSD), End of Season Date (EOSD), Date of Maximum PPI (MAXD), PPI values at start/end/peak (SOSV, EOSV, MAXV, MINV), Amplitude (AMPL), Season Length (LENGTH), Green-up and Senescence rates (LSLOPE, RSLOPE), Seasonal Productivity (SPROD), and Total Productivity (TPROD).

Provenance

Produced by VITO (Flemish Institute for Technological Research) under contract to the European Environment Agency (EEA) as part of the Copernicus Land Monitoring Service. Input: Sentinel-2 L2A atmospherically corrected surface reflectance. Processing chain: PPI computation → cloud/shadow filtering → dekadal compositing → Whittaker smoothing → phenological parameter extraction.

Classification System

Continuous phenological metrics (dates as day-of-year, PPI values 0-6, productivity as integrated PPI)

Accuracy Assessment

Validation based on comparison with BBCH phenological observations from PEP725 network across Europe. Start-of-season date RMSE typically 10-20 days depending on crop type and region. Peak greenness timing accuracy ~5-10 days. Higher accuracy for cropland and grassland than for forest understory. See CLMS quality assessment reports for detailed validation.
