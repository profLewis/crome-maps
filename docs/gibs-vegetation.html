MODIS NDVI & EVI (NASA GIBS)

Executive Summary

Global vegetation indices from the MODIS instrument aboard NASA's Terra satellite, served as 16-day composites via the Global Imagery Browse Services (GIBS). NDVI and EVI provide complementary measures of vegetation greenness and photosynthetic activity from 2000 to present at 250m-1km resolution.

Description

The MODIS MOD13A2 product provides two vegetation indices: NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) and EVI (Enhanced Vegetation Index). NDVI is the classical greenness index computed from red and near-infrared reflectance. EVI adds blue-band correction for atmospheric effects and has improved sensitivity in high-biomass regions (tropical forests) and reduced saturation compared to NDVI. In the viewer, mid-July composites are shown by default for each year, representing peak Northern Hemisphere growing season. GIBS serves pre-rendered map tiles at global coverage.

Provenance

MODIS Terra satellite (launched 1999), operated by NASA. Level 3 vegetation index products processed by the Land Processes Distributed Active Archive Center (LP DAAC) at USGS EROS. 16-day Maximum Value Composites from daily observations. GIBS tile service operated by NASA's Earth Observing System Data and Information System (EOSDIS).

Classification System

Continuous indices: NDVI range -0.2 to 1.0 (typical vegetation 0.2-0.9); EVI range -0.2 to 1.0 (typical vegetation 0.2-0.7)

Accuracy Assessment

MODIS NDVI/EVI are mature, well-validated products with 20+ years of heritage. Radiometric accuracy: ±0.02 for NDVI over stable targets. Known limitations: cloud contamination (mitigated by compositing), atmospheric effects (EVI more robust than NDVI), BRDF effects at high sun/view angles. See MODIS product validation status at LP DAAC.
