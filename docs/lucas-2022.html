LUCAS 2022 Copernicus

Executive Summary

EU-wide ground truth land cover survey with ~150,000 in-situ observation polygons across all 27 member states. Provides 82 detailed land cover classes verified by field surveyors, making it the primary validation dataset for European land cover mapping. Key reference for accuracy assessment of satellite- derived crop maps.

Description

LUCAS (Land Use and Coverage Area frame Survey) is a harmonized in-situ land cover/use survey conducted by Eurostat across the EU. The 2022 Copernicus module provides extended coverage with detailed polygon delineations and additional attributes for Copernicus product validation.

Provenance

Conducted by Eurostat with JRC technical support. Field surveyors visit stratified random sample points across the EU, recording land cover, land use, and environmental variables. The 2022 campaign added a Copernicus module with radial polygon delineation around survey points.

Classification System

LUCAS 82-class land cover classification (hierarchical: 8 L1 classes → 35 L2 → 82 L3)

Accuracy Assessment

LUCAS is itself a ground truth dataset used to validate other products. Positional accuracy of survey points is ±2m (GNSS). Classification accuracy depends on surveyor training and accessibility of sites. Quality control includes re-surveys at 5-10% of points.
